Digital Health Passport

Portable QR-based Health ID — Unified, secure, and portable

Core segments of the Digital Health Passport

QR-Based Health ID

Unique, portable QR codes — can be printed or kept in a mobile wallet. Stores emergency data on-scan and fetches full records securely online.

Interoperability (FHIR/HL7)

Adopts FHIR standards for exchanging medical data between hospitals, labs, and public health registries nationwide.

Role-Based Access

Granular access controls for Patients, Doctors, Labs and Admins. Consent-driven sharing ensures privacy and auditability.

Security & Privacy

End-to-end encryption, JWT/OAuth2 authentication, and patient consent tokens protect sensitive health data at rest and in transit.

Offline Emergency Data

Critical details (blood type, allergies, last vaccination) encoded on the QR for offline access during emergencies.

Sustainability & SDG Alignment

Minimizes paper usage, improves access to care, and directly supports SDG 3 — Good Health and Well-being.
